Sierpinski's Triangle is a mathematical phenomenon where a specific pattern results in a fractal figure within a triangle. In this demonstration, you may click anywhere on this page and the pattern will commence. From your chosen point, a random vertex of the triangle will be chosen, and a dot will be placed halfway between your chosen point and that vertex. Then, another vertex will be chosen, and a dot will appear halfway between the last dot and that vertex, and so forth. The pattern revealed is the fractal called Sierpinski's triangle. This fractal will occur no matter where the first dot is placed. Try it yourself, and enjoy.
